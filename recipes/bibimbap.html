Bibimbap

Bibimbap is a Korean rice dish. The term "bibim" means mixing rice (burned rice at the bottom of the dish and cooked rice), while the "bap" noun refers to rice. Bibimbap is served as a bowl of warm white rice topped with namul (saut√©ed and seasoned vegetables) or kimchi (traditional fermented vegetables) and gochujang (chili pepper paste), soy sauce, or doenjang (a fermented soybean paste). A raw or fried egg and sliced meat (usually beef) are common additions. The hot dish is stirred together thoroughly just before eating.[3]

    Ingredients

    Meat and Meat Sauce

  • 100g / 3.5 ounces beef mince (or other cuts)
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1/4 tsp minced garlic

    • Vegetables and Others

  • 250g (0.6 pounds) seasoned spinach
  • 50g (0.8 pounds) seasoned bean sprouts
  • 100g (3.5 ounces) shiitake mushroom
  • 120g (4.2 ounces) carrots (1 small)
  • 1/4 tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 to 4 serving portions of steamed rice
  • 3 or 4 eggs
  • cooking oil
  • Some toasted seasoned seaweed

    Steps

  1. Mix the bibimbap sauce ingredients in a bowl.
  2. Cook spinach and bean sprouts per linked recipe.
  3. Rinse, peel and julienne the carrots. Add some cooking oil and 1/4 tsp of fine sea salt in a wok and cook the carrots on medium high to high heat for 2 to 3 mins.
  4. Clean/rinse the shiitake mushrooms and thinly slice them. Add some cooking oil and 1/4 tsp of fine sea salt in a wok and cook the mushrooms on medium high to high heat until they are all cooked. (It takes 2 to 3 mins.)
  5. Make fried eggs.
  6. Put the rice into a bowl and add the meat, assorted vegetables, seasoned seaweed, bibimbap sauce, and the egg on top of the rice. Serve.
  7. To eat, mix the ingredients in the bowl, and enjoy!
